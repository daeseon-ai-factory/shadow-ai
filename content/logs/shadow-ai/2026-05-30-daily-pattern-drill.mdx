The request was specific and emotional: *not* phrasal verbs — the **patterns**. The frames a native uses like breathing — `as long as ___`, `I should have + p.p.`, question forms like `Have you ever…?` and `What if…?` — where the structure stays fixed and you just swap the words inside. And the demand: drill them **hard, every single day**, until they're automatic.

So: a **Pattern Drill**.

The unit is a frame, grouped by category — Questions, Modals & regret, Conditionals, Reactions, Opinions. Each frame carries a few real items: a Korean cue (the meaning to express) and a natural English model. The loop is built for production, not recognition:

1. You see the **frame** (`I should have ___`) and a **Korean cue** (the meaning).
2. You **say the full English out loud** — that's the rep.
3. **Reveal** a model answer, **self-mark** Got it / Again, next.

Same frame, new words, fast — the classic substitution drill, which is exactly how you make a pattern automatic. The Korean is only the *prompt*; the answer is always English. Self-marking (not auto-grading) because real production has many right answers — the model is one good one, your ear is the judge.

The piece that makes "every day" real: a **daily rep counter + a day streak** in localStorage, that advances when you practice on a new day and continues if you practiced yesterday. You see `🔥 3-day streak · 18 today`. Streaks are the cheapest, most honest habit mechanic there is.

v1 is curated — I wrote the patterns (real, idiomatic, nothing faked). The natural next step is mining patterns from the user's own clips, the same way the prepositions hub does: the frames you actually hear in the dev talks you watch become the frames you drill. Frontend-only for now; no backend needed to start swinging.
