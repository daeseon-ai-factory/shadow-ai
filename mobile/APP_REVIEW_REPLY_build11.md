## 2. Guideline 2.1(b) — Information Needed (business model)

The app is **completely free. There is no In-App Purchase, no subscription, and no paid
content of any kind.** Answering Apple's four questions directly:

> **1. Who are the users that will use the paid content, subscriptions, features, and services in the app?**
>
> There are no paid users. Anyone who creates a free account has full access to every
> feature at no cost.

> **2. Where can users purchase the content, subscriptions, features, and services that can be accessed in the app?**
>
> Nowhere. Nothing in the app is for sale. There is no purchase flow, no paywall, and no
> external purchase link presented to users.

> **3. What specific types of previously purchased content, subscriptions, features, and services can a user access in the app?**
>
> None. There is no previously purchased content to restore. The app has no purchase
> history concept.

> **4. What paid content, subscriptions, or features are unlocked within the app that do not use In-App Purchase?**
>
> None. Every feature — importing YouTube videos, clipping, shadowing, speaking practice,
> word-by-word notes, and spaced-repetition review — is available to all users for free.
>
> The **"FREE" badge** shown on the account screen is an **informational status label
> only**; it does not gate any feature and is not tied to any purchase. There is no "PRO"
> upgrade button, no pricing screen, and no link out to a website to pay.

If a paid tier is introduced in a future version, it will use the appropriate Apple
payment mechanism and we will submit it for review at that time.
